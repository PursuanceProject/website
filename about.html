About Pursuance and the Pursuance Project

What is Pursuance?

Pursuance allows participants to create and apply to join pursuances: customized, dynamic entities that look and feel much like living organizational charts, but with vastly greater potential for self-organization. These visualized structures, built out of the participants themselves, exist together in an online environment that's designed to facilitate collaboration among individuals and existing organizations.

Who can use Pursuance?

Since Pursuance is free and open source software, instances of it can be established by anyone, anywhere, for any imaginable activity. But the main server on which we'll be establishing the global, parallel civic ecosystem that is the ultimate purpose of this project is populated by two methods: applications to the core team (which continually reviews new applications as they come in and accepts appropriate candidates), and processional invitation (wherein each new participant is allowed to bring on some number of additional participants over time). All participants must sign on to a statement of ideals asserting that they oppose the surveillance state, police state, drug war, and efforts to restrict marriage rights due to gender, as understood by the participant. This is largely a formality, intended to convey that this is not a content-neutral platform, but rather an effort to establish a distinct polity possessing shared values.
